A Brief History

The Roman army existed in Europe from 753 BC to 1453 AD. What began as small scale raids developed into a national armed forces. At its peak in 200 AD, the Roman army had 33 legions of over 5,000 men. These volunteers went through a rigorous training program to make the Roman war machine the most efficient, obediant, and effective army of the time. Unfortunatly, through the combination of invading barbarians, poor hygeine, lead poisioning, and the lack of a gene pool, Rome fell in 476 AD.
