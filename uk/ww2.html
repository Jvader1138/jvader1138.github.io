The British Military of World War II

The Second War to End All Wars

A Brief History

With the fall of France, England saw itself fighting a war all by itself. It held out due to the Royal Navy and the RAF, and liberated France with help from America and Canada. Overall, around 2.9 million served in the British Army during the war.

Infantry Organization

In the British Army, sections contained 2 groups: the rifle group (7 men, lead by a corporal) and the gun group (3 men, lead by a lance corporal). The gun group carried 1 Bren Light Machine Gun (or "Bren gun" / "Bren"). About 3 of these sections were grouped together to form a platoon, lead by a lieutenant (or 2nd lieutenant) and a sergeant. The platoon also had a mortar team (using 2-inch mortar) and an anti-tank team (using PIAT). 3 platoons were grouped together to form a company, lead by a major, captain, and company sergeant major. The company also had its own radio to comunicate with other companies and the battalion HQ. This was usually operated by the captain from the rear of the attack. Battalions (lead by a lieutenant colonel) consisted of 4 infantry companies and 1 support company. 3 Battalions were grouped together to form a brigade (lead by a brigadier). Divisions included 3 brigades, an artillery brigade (3 field regiments, 1 AT regiment, and one AA regiment), and assisting units (medical, engineering, servicing, and policing). Artillery Organization

Artillery regiments (led by a lieutenant colonel) were made up of 3 batteries of 8 guns. Batteries were lead by a major, and had a captain, 2 lieutenants, and a number of NCO's on staff. 2 troops made up a battery. They operated 4 guns each, and were lead by a captain, 2 lieutenants, and a number of NCO's.

Armored (Tank) Organization

An armored brigade included 3 armored regiments, a motorized infantry battalion, and a servicing company. Regiments were broken up into squadrons and troops.
